Has Formula1 become too predictable?

Format: In F1 each year a number of races(typically 20) are held and drivers are given points based on the position they finish. At the end of the year winners are declared according to this sum. People today are debating if it has become more predictable/boring than the past i.e. are the same people winning every race and championships or is there a nice competition.

Here I try to check the validity of complaints about F1 becoming too predictable in the recent years. All data from January 1, 1970 to September 6, 2018(including Italian grand prix) considered

For the first chart, I take all the individual race result and see their correlation with the end of the year championship standings. Since points are given out per race and just added over the season, the correlation should be positive. However, a higher value of it suggests moremore predictability(can be seen as drivers finishing at nearly the same positions) and a lower correlation would suggest the regular winners and podium finishers did not end up ahead everyone else.

As can be seen, the concentration towards the top has increased which would suggest the same drivers winning repeatedly

  • Aggregate correlations over entire year to check how close the championship was
  • Do a similar analysis for Qualifying which would show performance difference over 1 lap(I could not figure out how to tackle the changing formats over the years)
  • Do a similar analysis but with weighted correlation, since there is lesser interest in a battle for finishing 15th than the battle for finishing 1st

For the second chart, I took a count for "How many times did x finish inside top 10 in this year?" and then averaged those values for a year. A higher average would suggest that same drivers are finishing in top 10 and there is less competition. As can be seen, that is the case. (Note that I removed backmarkers data for this). I also normalized the data for the number of races in the given year to count for the bias.

Conclusion: It seems that results today are more predictable. However, an increase in reliability of today's cars is also responsible for this and it remains to be seen how much the reliability aspect contributes to this.
